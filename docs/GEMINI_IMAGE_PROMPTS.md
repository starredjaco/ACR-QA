# Gemini / AI image prompts for the defense deck

## My honest take first — you need FEWER photos than your friend, not more

Your friend's stock-market project leans on photos because finance is a *visual* domain (charts,
trading floors, tickers) and his data is abstract. **Yours is the opposite: your strongest visuals
already exist and are real** — the live dashboard screenshots (Fleet, Run detail, OWASP, Attestation),
the architecture diagram, and the precision funnel. Real screenshots of a working system beat any
AI-generated image for credibility, and a defense committee rewards substance.

So the rule: **use AI images only as atmosphere on the title and section dividers — never to carry
information.** 1 hero on the title + 1 per section divider is the ceiling. Past that, decorative
"fake photos" on content slides will *lower* credibility ("why is there a random robot here?"). A judge
who sees a glossy stock photo next to a real metric trusts the metric less.

**Bottom line:** generate the 2 title heroes below (pick one), optionally 3 section-divider heroes.
Keep every content slide on real screenshots and diagrams.

---

## Generation tips (read once)
- Ask for **16:9 / 1920×1080**, "cinematic, high detail, no text, no words, no letters" (AI-rendered
  text always comes out garbled — we overlay the real title in PowerPoint).
- Palette to request every time: **deep navy (#002654) and gold (#C9A84C)** — matches the KSIU deck.
- Ask for a **darker composition with negative space on the left or center** so the title text sits
  cleanly on top.
- Generate 3–4 variations of each and pick the least cheesy. If it looks like a crypto ad, regenerate.

## SLIDE 1 — Title hero (generate both, pick one)

### Prompt A — "the trust gate" (recommended; matches your thesis metaphor)
```
A cinematic, premium 16:9 hero image for a cybersecurity software title slide. Deep navy blue
(#002654) background with subtle gold (#C9A84C) accents. Center-left: streams of softly glowing
source code flowing left-to-right, passing through a luminous translucent shield or checkpoint that
verifies and stamps each line with a small gold seal of approval. The verified code on the right side
glows clean gold; unverified code on the left is faint blue. Abstract, elegant, minimal, lots of dark
negative space on the upper-left for a title overlay. Soft volumetric light, shallow depth of field,
high detail, modern enterprise-tech aesthetic. NO text, NO words, NO letters anywhere in the image.
```

### Prompt B — "AI writes, we verify" (the problem-and-answer in one frame)
```
A cinematic 16:9 hero image for a graduation defense title slide about trust in AI-generated code.
Deep navy (#002654) background, gold (#C9A84C) highlights. On the left, an abstract glowing neural/AI
form emitting strands of code; on the right, a clean geometric vault or cryptographic seal where the
code is checked and locked. A clear visual flow from "generation" (organic, blue) to "verification"
(structured, gold). Dark, sophisticated, premium, plenty of empty dark space for an overlaid title.
Volumetric lighting, depth of field, no clutter. NO text, NO words, NO letters, NO numbers.
```

> Place it as a **full-bleed background** on slide 1 (send to back, ~30–40% opacity behind the navy)
> or as a **right-half panel** with the title on the left. Either keeps the title perfectly readable.

## What NOT to generate
- No fake screenshots, fake graphs, or fake numbers — you have real ones; fabricating data visuals is
  the one thing that can sink a defense on credibility.
- No generic "hacker in a hoodie" clichés.
- No AI-rendered logos or UI — the real dashboard is your UI.
